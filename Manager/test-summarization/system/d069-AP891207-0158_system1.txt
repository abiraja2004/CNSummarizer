In the Solidarity governments first definitive statement on the issue, Foreign Minister Krzysztof Skubiszewski told Parliament the two German states could be joined as part of the process of uniting Europe.
Western European countries have agreed to form an economic union by the end of 1992.
Skubiszewski faulted West German Chancellor Helmut Kohls program for gradual German reunification, offered last week, for not making clear that reunification would respect the existing border between Poland and East Germany.
Some right-wing West German politicians have called for recovering the 40,000 square miles of territory that Germany lost to Poland at the end of World War II.
