No breaches of major embankments or reservoirs were reported despite the most serious flood in a hundred years, effectively protecting the safety of the provincial capital, Guangzhou, and the Pearl (Zhujiang) River Delta.
While inspecting flood control and relief work in Qingyuan city on the Beijiang River yesterday, he attributed Guangdongs success in combating this flood -- almost the biggest in 100 years -- to concerted efforts by the armymen stationed in Guangdong and local residents.
According to him, 11 million people in the provinces nine cities and 55 counties were affected, and more than 200 people died in the natural disaster, with 189,000 rooms destroyed and 1.2 million hectares of crops ruined.
