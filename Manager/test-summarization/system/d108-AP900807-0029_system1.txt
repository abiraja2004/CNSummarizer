Iranian editorial writers are rejoicing now that the world is waking up to one of their most hated bogeymen: Iraqi President Saddam Hussein.
Iran frequently accuses both the U.S. and Saddam of trying to undermine the Islamic revolution.
In fact one of his reasons for invading Kuwait on Thursday, the Iraqi press noted, was its gall in demanding back tens of billions of dollars loaned to Iraq during the war Saddam says he fought for all Arabs.
The status of Great Satan was reserved for the United States, the all-encompassing public enemy No.
Under the Frankenstein headline, IRNA said in its own commentary: For the time being, tiny Kuwait ... breathes no more.
