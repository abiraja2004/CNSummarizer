All Salvation Army centers in the Bay Area, including 34 community service centers and scores of other units, have been turned over to the relief effort.
The Red Cross sent 290 units of blood from Los Angeles; the San Diego Blood Bank rushed 50 units to San Francisco within an hour of the quake; more than 400 units were sent from Portland, Ore. Giving makes them feel good, said Trudy Sullivan, a Red Cross spokeswoman in Portland.
Israel offered the services of a special army rescue squad that also helped dig out victims in Armenia.
The Osaka, Japan, city government announced it was sending $10,000 to San Francisco, a sister city since 1957.
