A spokesman for the Jammu-Kashmir state government said the situation was peaceful and absolutely normal in Srinagar, the states largest city.
Military jeeps carry senior government officials and civil servants around Srinagar in these dangerous days, as soldiers and paramilitary troopers try to stop a campaign of violence by Moslem separatists in the Kashmir Valley.
It is the valleys heavily Moslem population that tilts the sectarian scale in Jammu-Kashmir state.
The residents are constantly bombarded with Islamic sermons and are reminded that fighting a jihad (Islamic holy war) is the most sacred duty of every Moslem, the federal official said, on condition of anonymity.
