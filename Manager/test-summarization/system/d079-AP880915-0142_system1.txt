In the Yucatan state capital of Merida, the storms 160 mph winds destroyed nearly all the thatched houses in one district, uprooted trees, road signs and cut off water supplies, said Gonzalez Correa, news editor of Novedades.
At 3 p.m. today, the center of the storm was near latitude 22.3 north and longitude 92.9 west, or about 380 miles southeast of Brownsville, Texas, the National Weather Service said.
The storm was headed west northwest, said National Hurricane Center director Bob Sheets.
About 20,000 people were evacuated from Puerto Progreso and other coastal towns, a spokesman for the Interior Ministry in Mexico City said by telephone today.
