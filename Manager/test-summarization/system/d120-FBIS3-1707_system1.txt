Welcoming British Prime Minister John Major in Pittsburgh, where majors grandfather and father once lived, Clinton said at the airport, Were working together today to respond to the terrible tragedy in Bosnia to try to bring an end to the killing and to bring peace and to keep that conflict from spreading.
Major will spend a night at the White House, the first foreign head of state to have this honor since Clinton became President.
Major said Clinton would visit Britain, and perhaps the Oxford University, Clintons alma mater, during the June visit.
The two will also discuss Northern Ireland and what to do with NATO, Clinton said.
