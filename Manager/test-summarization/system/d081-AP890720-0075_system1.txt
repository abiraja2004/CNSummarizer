State television said Wednesday that the strike in the Donetsk basin had spread to 70 of its 120 mines, more than triple the previous number, and that mine construction workers had joined.
In a report from the Donetsk city of Yenakiyevo, television said steel and coke-chemical plants were on the verge of a stoppage for lack of coal.
But the newspaper and Tass reported more disturbances in the Ukraine.
In Karaganda, strikers said they walked out to protest incomplete news accounts of miners demands in the Kuznetsk strikes, according to Tass.
Komsomolskaya Pravda quoted a communist official in the Donetsk city of Makeyevka as saying, The scale of the strike is growing.
