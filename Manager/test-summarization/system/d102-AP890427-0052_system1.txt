At 15, Miss Ball worked as a barker at the amusement park, screaming for people to buy popcorn and hot dogs until she lost her voice.
On May 20, Miss Ball was to receive an honorary degree from the college and had planned to return to Jamestown, now a city of about 40,000.
Future generations in Celoron will have a reminder of their famous resident every time they drive or walk down the street where she grew up _ it will be renamed Lucy Lane.
After Miss Ball suffered a heart attack last week, residents decided to sign the posters and send them to her as a get-well greeting.
