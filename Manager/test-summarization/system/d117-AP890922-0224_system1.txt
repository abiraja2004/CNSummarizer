This years six nominees were chosen Thursday from 102 books considered by a panel headed by David Lodge, a British literary critic and novelist who was nominated for the prize last year for his book, Nice Work.
The other books nominated are: _ Cats Eye, a story of painter Elaine Risley and her return to her childhood home in Toronto by Canadian author Margaret Atwood, also a previous nominee.
The award, to be announced Oct. 26, carries a $31,600 prize funded by the Booker McConnell food company.
_ Jigsaw, by Sybille Bedford, an autobiographical novel about an adolescents travels through Germany, Italy and France.
